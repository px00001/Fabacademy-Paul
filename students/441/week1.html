Index.lock

Back home I go to help my wife who is recovering nicely. I decide to do a little more research on the Git that won't Git. I remember taking a course on distributed computing from Steve Garland while at Dartmouth, where he taught me the premise that every distributed system works on a public key and a locking strategy. I dug around a little further and discovered there is a file called index.lock which locks your directory while it is present. This prevents anything from working in your repository. I used GitLab to delete the file, and everything seemed good in Hinckley, Ohio the rest of that evening. I completed my index.html file and my aboutme.html file with OpenOffice, an open source office suite which is free to download, allows you to save as a HTML file, and does a half decent job with pictures.

Final Project(s) – The FDC and the Wine Tank Headspace Manager

One final project I would like to do is a device that can monitor the headspace of a wine tank and control the addition of purge gas (compressed Nitrogen, food grade). Another final project I would like to try is a rugged chassis for embedded computers such as a Raspberry Pi. I will call this a Field Deployable Computer, or FDC. Specifications would be weathertight, possibly mount as a riser box to an existing PVC eletrical pull box. It will have metal knobby terminals so you can attach wires from a lead acid battery or other low voltage source. It will have an IEEE 802.15.4 Xbee radio and communicate to a gateway box. The waterproof cover will hold the application specific sensor, input or output device, such as camera, moisture sensor, gas sensor, thermocouple junction, etc... The chassis should allow for two dry contacts. The FDC may be able to be configured for Wine Tank Headspace Manager.
